Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1937-2015

Missle Man of India

Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, and passed away on July 27, 2015. He is best known for his work on the Indian space program and his role in the development of India's missile capabilities, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India." Kalam was also a prolific author and a passionate advocate for education and youth empowerment.

Biography-section

  • 1931: Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • 1954: Graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.
  • 1960: Graduated in Aeronautical Engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).
  • 1960-1969: Worked at the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • 1969-1992: Joined the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and played a key role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 1992-1999: Served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).
  • 1998: Played a pivotal role in India's nuclear tests at Pokhran, which established India as a nuclear power.
  • 2002-2007: Served as the 11th President of India, known for his humility and dedication to public service.
  • 2015: Passed away on July 27 while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong.
